About Selena

    Selena Gomez, in full Selena Marie Gomez, (born July 22, 1992, Grand Prairie, Texas, U.S.), American actress and singer who won legions of young fans as the winsome star of the Disney television series Wizards of Waverly Place (2007–12) and as a pop vocalist. Soon she got attracted towards the music industry and formed the pop-rock band ‘Selena Gomez & the Scene’ through her record deal with Hollywood Records and released their debut studio album in August 2009.
    Selena engaged as an philanthropist and activist: she participated in Jude’s Children’s Hospital “Runway For Life” benefit in October 2008. Named as UNICEF’s spokesperson in the same year, for the Trick-or-Treat for UNICEF campaign, which aimed to encourage children to raise money on Halloween to help children around the world. As a result, named UNICEF’s spokesperson in 2009 and 2010 too. In August 2009, Selena Gomez (17-year-old) became the youngest UNICEF ambassador ever. In September 4 2009, which was her first official field mission, she traveled to Ghana for a week to witness the vulnerable conditions of children that basically lacked vital necessities such as clean water, nourishment, education and healthcare.
    Gomez won three Teen Choice Awards for her lead role in Wizards of Waverly Place. She also won the Kids’ Choice Award in the category of Favorite TV Actress, five times in a row. In 2003, for her social work efforts, she received the ‘Chris Greicius Celebrity Award’ from the ‘Make-A-Wish Foundation’, and the ‘Young Visionary Award’ at the ‘Unite4: Humanity’ ceremony.
    The lead single “Come & Get It” won the Choice Break-Up Song at the 2013 Teen Choice Awards, and the Best Pop Video at the 2013 MTV Video Music Awards. In the year 2015, Selena received the Chart-Topper award at the Billboard Women in Music event. At the 2016 People’s Choice Awards, Gomez won Favorite Animated Movie Voice for Hotel Transylvania 2. In 2016, Gomez received ‘American Music Awards’ for Favorite Pop/Rock Female Artist. In the same year, she achieved seven ‘Guinness World Records’.
    In 2017, Gomez teamed with Coach to launch the Selena Grace line of handbags and accessories, and the following year the two sides collaborated again for a ready-to-wear collection. In summer 2019, it was reported that the artist had filed a trademark for "Selena Gomez" for a series of beauty products, including fragrances, skin-care and hair-care preparations, cosmetics, soaps and essential oils.
    In May 2020, it was announced Gomez would host and executive produce the HBO Max cooking show Selena + Chef, which features Gomez joined by a different chef remotely each episode due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Each episode highlights a food-related charity. The show premiered on August 13, 2020.
    In August 2020, Gomez collaborated with South Korean girl group Blackpink for "Ice Cream", the song is the second single from Blackpink's debut Korean-language studio album, The Album. In August 2020, it was announced that Gomez will star in and executive produce the Hulu comedy series Only Murders In the Building alongside Steve Martin and Martin Short. The project marks Gomez's first scripted television role since Wizards of Waverly Place.
